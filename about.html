About

Why The Field Report exists

Public health education is largely American. The research shaping the rest of the world rarely makes it into our classrooms — and when it does, it is rarely made accessible to people outside the field.

The Field Report is a student-led publication at NYU School of Global Public Health that translates peer-reviewed global health research into accessible, skills-based writing. Every brief is written by a student, reviewed by a faculty member, and published biweekly.

The goal is twofold: to surface research from underrepresented geographies, and to give students a real, faculty-reviewed writing sample for their job applications.

Editorial Standards

Every brief undergoes faculty review before publication. Briefs represent student academic work and are not peer-reviewed journal publications. Authors are credited alongside their program and faculty reviewer.

The Team

The Field Report is run by students at NYU School of Global Public Health. If you're interested in writing, reviewing, or supporting the publication, visit the Contribute page.
